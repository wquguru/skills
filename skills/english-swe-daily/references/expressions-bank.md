# Expressions Bank — English for SWEs

A curated reference of natural expressions organized by topic. Draw from these during sessions, supplement with your own, and avoid repeating ones already covered in-conversation.

---

## 🎙️ Standup Updates

### Giving progress

- "I wrapped up X yesterday and I'm picking up Y today."
- "Still in the middle of X — should have it done by end of day."
- "Made some good progress on X. Hit a snag with Y, but I think I've got it figured out."
- "I'm pretty close on X — just need to clean it up and open a PR."
- "Yesterday was mostly meetings, but I'm back on X today."

### Mentioning blockers

- "One thing blocking me is X — I might need some help from [person]."
- "I'm a bit stuck on X. Could someone point me in the right direction after this?"
- "Waiting on a review from [person] before I can move forward."
- "I've got a question about the design for X — can we sync after standup?"

### Keeping it short and natural

- "That's it from me."
- "Nothing blocking me — good to go."
- "Same as yesterday, still heads-down on X."

## 🙋 Asking for Help / Raising Blockers

### Asking in Slack

- "Hey [name], got a sec to look at something?"
- "Quick question — do you know if X is expected behavior or a bug?"
- "Not sure if I'm missing something, but X isn't working the way I'd expect. Mind taking a look?"
- "I've been banging my head against this for a while — any chance you have context on X?"
- "Would it be okay to grab 10 minutes to talk through X?"

### Escalating gracefully

- "Just wanted to flag that X is taking longer than expected — might affect the deadline."
- "Heads up — I ran into an issue with X that might need input from the team."
- "Not sure this is the right call — would love a second opinion before I move forward."

### Thanking for help

- "That was super helpful, thanks!"
- "Oh, that makes sense — appreciate you explaining that."
- "Good to know — I'll go with that approach."

## 💬 Code Feedback (PR Comments & Reviews)

### Giving constructive feedback

- "Not a blocker, but have you considered X? Might be cleaner."
- "This looks good overall — one small thing: X"
- "Could we move this to a helper function? Might make it easier to test."
- "Just a nit: X" (nit = minor/nitpicky comment, very common in code reviews)
- "I wonder if X would be simpler here — happy to discuss if you had a reason for the current approach."

### Approving warmly

- "Looks good to me! 🚀" (LGTM)
- "Nice work — this is much cleaner than before."
- "Happy to approve — left one small comment but it's not a blocker."

### Responding to feedback on your own code

- "Good catch, fixing that now."
- "Ah yeah, that was sloppy — thanks for flagging."
- "I actually went with X because of Y — does that make sense, or do you still think we should change it?"
- "Fair point. I'll refactor that."

## 🤝 Disagreeing Politely / Pushing Back

### Softening disagreement

- "I see where you're coming from, but I'm a bit worried about X."
- "That could work — I guess my concern is X."
- "I might be wrong, but wouldn't X cause a problem with Y?"
- "Just thinking out loud — what if we tried X instead?"
- "I hear you. I just want to make sure we've thought about X."

### Proposing alternatives

- "What if we went with X? Might be easier to maintain."
- "Another option could be X — curious what you think."
- "Could we try X first and revisit if it doesn't work?"

### Agreeing to disagree / committing

- "I still have some reservations, but I'm happy to try it your way."
- "Fair enough — let's go with that."
- "I'll defer to you on this one."

## ☕ Casual Small Talk

### Monday / start of week

- "How was your weekend?"
- "Did you get up to anything fun?"
- "Pretty quiet weekend for me — just recharged."
- "Went by way too fast, honestly."

### Reacting to news / team updates

- "Oh nice, congrats!"
- "Oh no, that's rough."
- "Wait, seriously? That's wild."
- "Ugh, that's frustrating — sorry to hear that."

### Wrapping up a conversation

- "Anyway, I'll let you get back to it."
- "Okay, I'll stop bugging you — thanks again!"
- "Alright, catch you later."

### Filling silence or buying thinking time (spoken)

- "That's a good question — let me think about that for a sec."
- "Hmm, so basically…"
- "I mean, yeah, I think…"
- "Fair point, I hadn't thought of it that way."
